About

I love UX because it allows me to wear different hats, as an interface designer, researcher, information architect, and interaction designer. Over the years I have developed my skillsets in interface design, in both physical and digital space.

My industrial design background provides me with a strong grounding in Human Centred Design, including problem solving and stakeholder management. Since graduation I've worked as a visual designer across graphic, branding, and interaction design fields. I’m comfortable working in multidisciplinary environments and throughout all phases of the design process.

During my free time, I love learning how to code and checking out new design tools. I also enjoy holding creative sessions on brush calligraphy, hand-made crafts, and watercolour paintings.

Why #Notjustapotato ?

I have a potato that I’ve been carrying around everywhere, everyday, since I was 17.
Why the potato? To be honest, I don’t have a reason for that.
However, over the years, I have learnt a lot from my potato.

"Potatoes are generalists. They can be chips, hash brown, mashed, or starch.“

The potato has inspired me to be diverse and adaptable. Potato can come in different forms when it needs to be, while still remains its essence. Just as there are multiple flavours of chips on the shelf, there are also multiple ways to think creatively and solve problems.
Next time when you see me, remind me to introduce you to my potato.

MY DESIGN APPROACH

Experience mapping

To begin with, I would start with user research and envision the kind of experience I would like to bring to the user.

Prototype Crafting

I then would roll out my wireframing dough to make a quick and rough prototype for discussion, making sure everyone is on the same page.

User Testing

Testing with the user helps me to understand whether or not I have achieved my goal and also gaining feedback for further development.

Final refinement

Then on the final stage I would spice it up with more details, and make sure everything works nicely!
